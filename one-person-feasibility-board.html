ShipFast

About $250K in roughly 5 months at ~90% margin selling a code boilerplate, per the founder's own newsletter.

可複製模式: A digital kit sells when it saves a specific buyer hours on a job they already committed to, and the founder publishes proof of the product working.

OpenClaw 適配: Medium fit: our paid kits match the format, but ShipFast converted through founder trust; our kits still lack any public traffic evidence.

限制: The boilerplate market is crowded and audience-driven; OpenClaw should not expect kit sales without a distribution surface that shows the kits working.

OpenClaw 今日指令

  • CEO/Strategy: Use this board as the owner-proxy feasibility read: Listing Audit request pack first, Mood Anchor trust asset second, no complex SaaS until demand repeats.
  • Revenue Ops: Keep Listing Friction Grader, sample proof, and Listing Audit request pack ready for owner-reviewed qualified inquiries.
  • Product Studio QA: Keep Mood Anchor PWA and Mood Anchor care card safe, local-only, printable, RWD, and non-medical.
  • Marketing Desk: Prepare channel-safe owner-reviewed copy around useful free tools, not income promises or automatic posting.

  • Support/Ops: Enforce external-authority boundaries, no sensitive server collection, and low-memory runtime guardrails.

停止線

  • No external authority actions: no login, listing creation, public posting, DMs, emails, scheduling, checkout setup, payment, payout, tax, refund, credential, price, discount, or account changes.
  • No certainty claims for revenue, traffic, conversion, ranking, platform approval, customer acquisition, productivity, mood, treatment, crisis response, or health outcomes.
  • No server collection of sensitive Mood Anchor data, private listing text, credentials, medical information, customer records, or private analytics.
  • No OpenClaw message commands, browser relay dependence, direct runtime control, long-lived agents, scraping, platform bypassing, or automatic channel actions.
