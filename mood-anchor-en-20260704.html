An offline, local-only mood support tool for choosing one tiny next step on a low day.

If you are not safe right now, contact a real person first

This is not therapy, treatment, or a crisis service. If you might harm yourself, do not want to live, or feel like you may lose control, use human support before using this tool.

  • Immediate danger: call your local emergency number now: If you may act soon, have a plan or means, or need urgent medical help, move away from danger and seek in-person help now.
  • United States: call, text, or chat 988: The 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline offers free, confidential support by call, text, or chat.
  • Canada: call or text 9-8-8: 9-8-8 provides suicide crisis support across Canada by phone or text, 24 hours a day.
  • United Kingdom and Ireland: call Samaritans at 116 123: If you need urgent emotional support in the United Kingdom or Ireland, call Samaritans at 116 123 any time.
  • Other countries: Find a Helpline: Use Find a Helpline to locate verified local emotional support by country, topic, phone, text, or chat.
  • Trusted person: send one short message: Try: "I do not feel safe being alone right now. Can you stay with me or help me contact support?"

Safety sources

  • 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline: U.S. call, text, and chat support is available 24/7/365.
  • Canada 9-8-8: Canada offers 24/7/365 crisis support by calling or texting 9-8-8.
  • Canada.ca mental health crisis guidance: Canada.ca says to call 9-1-1 for immediate danger and 9-8-8 for suicide crisis support.
  • Samaritans phone support: Samaritans provides free phone support at 116 123 in the United Kingdom and Ireland.
  • Find a Helpline: Find a Helpline lists verified emotional support services across many countries.
  • NIMH Caring for Your Mental Health: NIMH lists self-care ideas such as movement, regular meals, hydration, sleep, relaxing activities, and staying connected.
  • NICE Depression in Adults Guidance: NICE guidance supports structured care and activity-focused approaches under proper clinical context.

Usage boundary

  • This is not therapy, diagnosis, treatment, crisis counseling, or medical advice.
  • This is not a crisis service and it cannot monitor your safety.
  • Do not use the scores as a medical assessment; they only help organize today.
  • If you have self-harm thoughts, want to die, or feel unsafe, use human crisis support first.
  • Your entries stay only in this browser localStorage; clearing browser data removes them.
