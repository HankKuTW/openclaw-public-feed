# Listing Friction Grader Channel Research - 20260704

Purpose: choose English-language distribution channels for `Listing Friction Grader` that can move qualified sellers toward the `$79 Digital Product Listing Audit` without auto-posting, spam, vote manipulation, or server collection of pasted listing text.

Current public asset:

- Tool: https://red-mole-94.loca.lt/listing-friction-grader-20260704.html
- Service offer: https://red-mole-94.loca.lt/service-revenue-offer-20260704.html
- Dashboard: https://red-mole-94.loca.lt/company-dashboard.html

## Source Findings

1. Product Hunt
   - Sources: Product Hunt Launch Guide, Product Hunt Community Guidelines.
   - Relevant rule: Product Hunt says promotion can ask people to visit and comment, but not directly ask for upvotes. Its community guidelines also prohibit mass messaging, bots, incentivized upvotes, and artificial activity.
   - Fit: medium. The tool is usable, but Product Hunt should wait until assets, maker profile, and positioning are polished.
   - Stop line: no upvote requests, no vote swaps, no mass messages, no bot traffic.

2. Hacker News Show HN
   - Source: Show HN Guidelines.
   - Relevant rule: Show HN is for something people can try, inspect, and discuss. Landing pages, fundraisers, and plain service pitches are off-topic. Users should be able to try it without barriers, and friends should not be asked to upvote or comment.
   - Fit: high for the free tool, low for a direct service pitch. Listing Grader is local-only and tryable without signup, so it can be framed as a small tool rather than an audit sales page.
   - Stop line: submit the tool, not the service offer; no upvote or comment requests.

3. Reddit
   - Sources: Reddit Rules, Reddiquette.
   - Relevant rule: Reddit requires community-specific rule review, authentic participation, no spam, no content manipulation, and no misleading identity or affiliation.
   - Fit: medium to low until the owner has chosen exact subreddits and reviewed each sidebar/rules page.
   - Stop line: no subreddit posting until the owner checks that community's rules; no DMs, vote requests, sockpuppets, or repeated link drops.

4. Indie Hackers
   - Source: Indie Hackers product update on group posting guidelines.
   - Relevant rule: Indie Hackers groups can have their own posting guidelines that appear before posting. Treat each group as separate, not as a blanket permission to promote.
   - Fit: medium. Best angle is a transparent build-in-public post asking for feedback on a free local-only tool.
   - Stop line: review group guidelines before posting; do not cross-post the same pitch into multiple groups.

5. Uneed
   - Sources: Uneed submit page and launch guide.
   - Relevant rule: Uneed accepts product submissions through a product address and prepares launch materials. Its launch guide emphasizes preparation of posts, visuals, and product story before launch day.
   - Fit: high for a simple launchpad listing because the tool has a public URL and clear user problem.
   - Stop line: owner must manually create/sign in and submit; no automated scraping/submission from OpenClaw.

6. SaaSHub
   - Source: SaaSHub submit page.
   - Relevant rule: SaaSHub positions itself as an independent software marketplace for alternatives and reviews.
   - Fit: medium. Submit only if the tool can be framed as a software/productivity utility, not as a service-only offer.
   - Stop line: no paid promotion or account setup without owner approval.

7. BetaList
   - Source: BetaList submit page.
   - Fit: low for the current asset because Listing Grader is already public and is not positioned as a private beta startup.
   - Stop line: do not spend effort here unless the tool becomes a broader beta product.

## Channel Priority

1. Show HN: highest immediate fit because the tool is tryable without signup.
2. Uneed: high fit as a launchpad/directory for a simple public product.
3. Indie Hackers: medium fit if framed as a founder learning/build post.
4. Reddit: medium to low until exact subreddit rules are checked.
5. Product Hunt: later, after assets and profile work.
6. SaaSHub: optional directory listing.
7. BetaList: defer.

## OpenClaw Direction

- Marketing Desk prepares owner-reviewed drafts only.
- Support/Ops checks every draft against each channel rule and the no-guarantee/no-server-storage boundary.
- Analytics records only owner-entered public metrics: visits, replies, audit requests, paid audits, objections, support questions, and repeated friction themes.
- Revenue Ops keeps the CTA toward the `$79 Digital Product Listing Audit`, but the free tool stays useful without requiring payment.
