# Mood Anchor Action Ladder Owner-Proxy Directive - 2026-07-04

Role: Codex is acting as the owner's proxy for internal OpenClaw direction. This directive is approved for local execution. Do not ask the owner for more approval unless the next step requires a real account, public post, payment, credential, private user data, or real-world commitment.

## Decision

Mood Anchor should move from a generic check-in toward a global English, local-only digital product that helps a low-energy user choose one tiny next step. The next product increment is a personal action ladder: primary step, smaller backup, and human-support handoff.

## Department Orders

1. CEO/Strategy
   - Treat Mood Anchor English as the current free trust-building product experiment.
   - Do not frame it as a depression treatment, therapy substitute, crisis service, or paid medical offer.
   - Keep revenue exploration limited to ethical adjacent resources after aggregate validation only.

2. Product Studio QA
   - Verify the zh-TW and English PWAs expose friction controls, support-style controls, primary step, smaller backup, and human handoff.
   - Confirm the PWA remains offline-capable and uses only browser `localStorage`.
   - Confirm no form action, tracking endpoint, account, or server analytics was added.

3. Support/Ops
   - Review crisis-first routing, no-diagnosis wording, no-treatment wording, and no-outcome-guarantee wording.
   - Confirm high-risk flags override the action ladder and route to human crisis resources.
   - Confirm low-energy/non-crisis states still show a clear human-support handoff line.

4. Marketing Desk
   - Use only non-medical positioning: "private offline mood-support PWA", "one tiny next step", "local-only check-in".
   - Do not claim depression treatment, suicide-prevention capability, clinical improvement, or replacement of professional support.
   - Prepare owner-reviewed drafts only; do not post, submit, DM, email, or schedule automatically.

5. Analytics
   - Measure only owner-entered aggregate channel signals such as visits, clarity replies, privacy-trust replies, and return-use intent.
   - Do not collect sensitive mood entries, safety flags, names, emails, health stories, or crisis details on a server.
   - If safety confusion appears, recommend copy/flow iteration before any wider distribution.

## Stop Line

No OpenClaw department may log in, publish, post, DM, email, create checkout, change price, collect sensitive health data on a server, or present medical/revenue outcomes as guaranteed. Keep all external authority with the human owner.
