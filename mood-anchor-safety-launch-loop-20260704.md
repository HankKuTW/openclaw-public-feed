## Department Orders

### CEO/Strategy

- Treat Mood Anchor as a free trust-building distribution asset, not a medical product.
- Use the first loop to test clarity, privacy confidence, and whether the 7 日微行動計畫 is easy to understand.
- Default success signal: 5 qualified opens and 2 clarity replies before any broader distribution.

### Product Studio QA

- Test mobile width before desktop polish.
- Confirm the PWA works after refresh, service worker registration is present, and localStorage is the only data store.
- Confirm the 7 日微行動計畫 can be created, checked off, and cleared with local data.

### Support/Ops

- Keep crisis and safety text above growth copy.
- Keep 988 visible for U.S. users and tell users to contact local emergency services for immediate danger.
- No sensitive-health-data collection: do not collect mood entries, self-harm flags, notes, exported JSON, or private stories on any server.

### Marketing Desk

- Use only non-medical language: private mood check-in, small support step, offline local-only PWA, next tiny action.
- Ask for UX and clarity feedback from owned channels only.
- Do not post in mental health support communities unless self-promotion is explicitly allowed.

### Analytics

- No server analytics.
- Track only manual aggregate signals: external page opens if already visible, direct replies, clarity feedback count, safety/confusion issues, and requests for an English version.
- Do not ask users to send mood history, check-in notes, self-harm flags, or exported JSON.

## First 24-Hour Loop

1. Product Studio QA opens the PWA on desktop and mobile width.
2. Support/Ops reviews crisis wording, no-treatment language, privacy copy, and 7 日微行動計畫 wording.
3. Marketing Desk prepares one low-key feedback post for an owned channel only.
4. CEO/Strategy decides whether the next iteration is English localization, better onboarding, a printable self-care card, or a clearer B2B wellness-team product.

## Stop Line

- No OpenClaw gateway, agent, runtime, browser relay, or message command use.
- No medical claims.
- No sensitive-health-data collection.
- No automated posting.
- No direct messages to vulnerable users.
