Support Message Scripts

Copy-ready messages for asking a trusted person for support on a low-energy day.

Before You Use This

This page only prepares text. You choose whether to copy it, where to paste it, and who receives it; it does not send messages or contact anyone for you.

  • This is not therapy, diagnosis, treatment, crisis counseling, or medical advice.
  • This page does not send messages, read contacts, email, DMs, or account data.
  • If you have self-harm thoughts, want to die, or feel unsafe right now, use human crisis support first.

If You Are Not Safe Right Now

  • Immediate danger: call your local emergency number now: If you may act soon, have a plan or means, or need urgent medical help, move away from danger and seek in-person help now.
  • United States: call, text, or chat 988: The 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline offers free, confidential support by call, text, or chat.
  • Canada: call or text 9-8-8: 9-8-8 provides suicide crisis support across Canada by phone or text, 24 hours a day.
  • United Kingdom and Ireland: call Samaritans at 116 123: If you need urgent emotional support in the United Kingdom or Ireland, call Samaritans at 116 123 any time.
  • Other countries: Find a Helpline: Use Find a Helpline to locate verified local emotional support by country, topic, phone, text, or chat.
  • Trusted person: send one short message: Try: "I do not feel safe being alone right now. Can you stay with me or help me contact support?"

Copy-Ready Messages

Let someone know today is hard

There is no immediate danger, but you do not want to handle the day alone.

I am having a really low day and wanted someone to know. You do not need to write a long reply. If you can, a short check-in would help.

Ask someone to stay connected

You can send a message, but being alone for too long does not feel good.

I am having a hard time right now. Could you stay with me for a bit, by phone, nearby, or by keeping a message thread open?

Ask for one practical next step

Food, water, leaving bed, making an appointment, or choosing a step feels too big.

I am struggling to break things down. Could you help me choose one tiny next step, or remind me to drink water, eat something small, or move to a safer spot?

Say you are not safe right now

You are worried you might harm yourself, lose control, or should not be alone.

I do not feel safe being alone right now. Can you stay with me, help me contact support, or help me use 988 or local emergency resources?
